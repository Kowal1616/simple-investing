Portfolios

Portfolios listed on SimpleInvesting where chosen following these principles:

  • Make investing as easy and low maintance as possible, so..
  • The less assets the better
  • Favor accumulating ETFs
  • Keep costs as low as possible

Global Technology

Embraces the future of innovation with Global Technology portfolio. This portfolio focuses on leading technology companies worldwide, offering exposure to the latest advancements in the tech sector.

USA (S&P 500)

The powerhouse of the global economy with USA portfolio, which tracks the S&P 500 index. Benefits from the growth potential of top U.S. companies across various sectors.

All World

Diversifies investments across the entire global market with All World portfolio. This diversified approach provides exposure to both developed and emerging markets, ensuring broad market coverage.

80-20 Stocks-Bonds

Lowered volatility with bonds gives the right balance between growth and stability with 80-20 Stocks-Bonds portfolio. This portfolio allocates 80% to stocks for growth potential and 20% to bonds for added stability in varying market conditions. [1]

60-40 Stocks-Bonds

More bonds for even lower volatility - a balanced approach to investing with 60-40 Stocks-Bonds portfolio. With a 60% allocation to stocks and 40% to bonds, this portfolio aims to optimize returns while lowering risk. [1]

Gold

Very old and well-known commodity. Gold has long been considered a safe-haven asset, offering stability and protection against economic uncertainties.


[1] For the stocks we chose a Developed World sector (23 developed countries) and for bonds European Corporate Bonds (European - to avoid currency risk, Corporate to maximise investor's profits).
